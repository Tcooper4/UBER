Panel regressions in N02-N05 use zone-day aggregated data rather than the
full hourly panel. The hourly panel (3.65M obs × 14,877 hourly periods)
generates a within-transformation matrix exceeding available RAM during
two-way FE estimation.

Aggregation: trip_count, fare, miles summed over hours within day;
fare_per_mile computed as ratio-of-sums on daily totals (consistent with
locked methodology); binary event flags = max() over hours (flagged if
ANY hour in day was flagged). Continuous weather: temp = mean, precip =
sum, wind = max.

Hour-level granularity is preserved in:
- Per-event lift analysis (N02 Cells 4-6, 10b, 12) via collapse-first
  baseline matching at (zone, hour-of-week) level
- Hour-of-week substitution correlation (N05 Cell 5)
- Holiday day-level vs same-DOW baseline (N02 Cell 11)

Daily aggregation is the standard panel-DiD specification in published
transportation economics (Hall, Kendrick & Nosko 2015; Cramer & Krueger
2016). The hour-level signal is recovered via the per-event analysis
where it matters most.
